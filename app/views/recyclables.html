COLLECTION

Two types of recyclables

Recyclables are separated by residents and public institutions into two categories: metal, glass and plastic (MGP); and paper.

The Department of Sanitation collects them either by using two different trucks, or dual-bin trucks such as this one.

Recycling paper in the city

About half of the paper collected by DSNY is recycled in the city, at the Pratt Paper Mill on Staten Island.*DSNY Recycling and Disposal Networks, 2016; NYSDEC Transfer Station Annual Reports, 2016.

The paper is taken there from Manhattan by barge, or from Staten Island and South Brooklyn by collection trucks.

Sorting facilities

Approximately 20% of the metal, glass, and plastic (MGP) collected is taken by trucks to a sorting facility in Jersey City.*DSNY Recycling and Disposal Networks, 2016; NYSDEC Transfer Station Annual Reports, 2016.

The other 80% of MGP, along with the other half of the paper that is not recycled in Staten Island, is taken to a sorting facility in Sunset Park, either by truck or barge.

Both facilities are owned by Sims Metal Management.

SORTING

Arriving at Sims Sunset Park

As DSNY trucks enter the Sims sorting facility, they are weighed on this truck scale. The data is sent automatically to DSNY and allows them to calculate the quantity of recyclables collected.

This data is used for two purposes. First, it allows DSNY to estimate what percentage of the waste is being recycled. Second, since Sims and DSNY have a contract where they share revenue from selling recyclables, it allows DSNY to keep track of how much money is being made.

TIPPING

Entering the facility

Paper

Paper recyclables are unloaded in one corner of the area known as the "tipping floor."

It will be loaded on semi trucks and sent to national or international paper recyclers.

Metal, glass, and plastic

The other part of DSNY's recyclables is composed of mixed metal, glass, and plastic.

These are tipped on an opposite corner of the facility for further sorting.

 

Front loaders will drop the recyclables onto a raising treadmill, which leads to the mechanized sorting system in the adjacent room.

Mechanized sorting
